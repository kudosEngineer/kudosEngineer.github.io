What drives me

I love that threat hunting is fundamentally a thinking problem. You start with nothing — no alert, no ticket, no obvious indicator — and you follow a hypothesis through telemetry until the story either holds or falls apart.

What excites me just as much is the other side: turning what you find into a detection that fires automatically the next time. Good detection engineering means the threat you hunted last week can never hide again. There's something deeply satisfying about closing that loop.

Why I write

Security knowledge has a hoarding problem. A lot of what practitioners learn — the real hunting techniques, the detection logic that actually works, the IR lessons from live incidents — stays locked inside company walls.

I write to push back against that. If something I've hunted, built, or broken helps someone level up, that's reason enough to put it down.
